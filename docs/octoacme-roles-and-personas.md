# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams. They gather, analyze, and refine requirements to ensure alignment between business needs and project deliverables.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear, actionable user stories and acceptance criteria
- Conduct gap analysis and process mapping
- Facilitate requirements workshops and validation sessions
- Ensure traceability from requirements to implementation
- Support UAT (User Acceptance Testing) planning and execution

### Goals
- Ensure project deliverables meet actual business needs
- Reduce rework by clarifying requirements early
- Maintain stakeholder alignment throughout the project lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user story definitions
- Stakeholder interviews and validation sessions
- Collaboration with Product Managers on prioritization
- Coordination with Developers on technical feasibility

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on product vision, prioritization, and success metrics
- **Project Managers**: Provide requirement clarity for project planning and scope management
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements, answer questions during implementation
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Define acceptance criteria and support test case development

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads oversee the testing process to ensure deliverables meet required standards and acceptance criteria. They establish quality gates and drive testing excellence across the team.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and approach for projects
- Create and maintain test plans and test cases
- Coordinate testing activities across the team
- Track and manage defects through resolution
- Ensure acceptance criteria are testable and validated
- Drive test automation initiatives
- Report on quality metrics and test coverage

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality deliverables that meet acceptance criteria
- Minimize production defects through comprehensive testing
- Improve testing efficiency through automation

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Defect reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Daily standup participation and sprint planning input
- Sign-off on release readiness

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on testability, review defects, and support root cause analysis
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and validate feature behavior
- **Business Analysts**: Ensure test cases align with requirements
- **Release Coordinator**: Provide quality sign-off for releases

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers coordinate changes to project scope, timelines, or resources. They act as the main point of contact for assessing, documenting, and communicating changes and their impacts to all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Evaluate change requests and their impact on scope, schedule, and resources
- Facilitate change control board meetings (if applicable)
- Document change decisions and maintain change log
- Communicate changes and impacts to all stakeholders
- Update project documentation to reflect approved changes
- Track cumulative impact of changes on project health

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from uncontrolled changes
- Ensure all stakeholders understand change impacts before approval
- Maintain project stability while enabling necessary adaptations

### Typical Communication
- Change request forms and impact assessments
- Change control board meeting facilitation
- Stakeholder notifications about approved changes
- Updated project plans reflecting changes

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Collaborate on impact assessment and project plan updates
- **Product Managers**: Evaluate change requests against product priorities and roadmap
- **Developers**: Assess technical feasibility and effort estimates for changes
- **Business Analysts**: Validate requirements changes and update documentation

---

## Release Coordinator

### Role Summary
Release Coordinators manage deployment activities, ensure readiness for releases, and communicate status to broader teams. They orchestrate the technical and organizational aspects of getting features into production.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases in coordination with development and operations teams
- Verify completion of pre-release requirements (tests, security scans, documentation)
- Coordinate deployment activities and communications
- Ensure rollback plans are in place
- Monitor post-deployment health and coordinate issue resolution
- Maintain release calendar and communicate timelines
- Document release notes and deployment procedures

### Goals
- Enable smooth, low-risk deployments to production
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Maintain clear communication about release status and timelines

### Typical Communication
- Release schedules and deployment windows
- Pre-release readiness reviews
- Release announcements and status updates
- Post-deployment summaries and metrics

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate release timing with project milestones and stakeholder expectations
- **Developers**: Ensure code is ready for deployment and verify technical readiness
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Confirm testing completion and quality sign-off
- **Operations/DevOps**: Coordinate deployment execution and monitoring
